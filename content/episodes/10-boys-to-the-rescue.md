“Boys, you need to help — I’m falling apart,” Baz’s muffled sentence came from behind a cushion.

“Watch how it goes.” came Max's response as he continued throwing a ball up and down, leaning back on a chair in Baz’s spacious Belgravia pad.

“Look, I don’t know what you were expecting,” Archie said. “The one thing you were asked not to do was fall in love with another girl!”

“I never fell in love,” Baz replied quickly.

“A love letter and a continuous stream of dates counts as love, Baz — in this situation.”

“Boy, Lily Allen sang it right — these open relationships are complicated,” sighed Basil.

“Relationships are a living thing. You need to give them room to breathe and grow. Each argument will either bring you closer together or push you apart. Play your next move carefully.”

“Eurgh, they’re probably on their way to Portofino by now,” Baz groaned, sinking further into the chair.

“Cabo,” Max corrected, still throwing the ball.

Basil jumped up, intercepted the ball, and collapsed again to lie out on the sofa. 

Archie tackled him. “If she’s worth it, don’t give up, Bazzy-Boy,” Archie bolstered.
