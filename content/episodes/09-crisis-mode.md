The girls gathered around, they were in crisis mode. 

“What about a holiday?” Mags tried 

“You just need something to eat, and this will all feel better,” G cooed. 

‘Rehash it- what did he say, exactly?’ Asked mags, again.

Bea had already done that though, for 45 minutes outside of The Gambit Bar as Basil had run after the departed Birdie. They had rehashed it so much that even the sight of a new handbag couldn't cheer Bea up now. 

‘Look we are going in circles. The fact is he broke my trust, so that’s that.’ Birdie said deadpan. Mags and G exchanged silent looks. 

‘So no more Birdie and Baz?’ G broke. 

“I guess there wasn’t really ever a Birdie and Baz, if this is how he saw our relationship - a total joke,” Birdie exclaimed, a quiver nearly cracking the sentence. 

‘Do you think he kissed her at the festival that time, and that’s why he was cagey with his phone?’ Flick shot G death eyes. 

‘He was at Glasto G! No one should be on their phone then!’ Bea replied, indignantly. 

'Hear him out again' G reasoned definitively 

'And I'll book Cabo!' cried an already executing Mags.
